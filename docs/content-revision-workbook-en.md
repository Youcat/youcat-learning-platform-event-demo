## Editorial principles

1. A wrong option should sound plausible, not foolish.
2. Questions should test distinctions, judgment, and application—not recognition of a slogan.
3. Scenario questions ask for the **best** response when several options contain something partly true.
4. Games rotate between five mechanics: crossword, match pairs, reveal cards, set into the right order, and move-block puzzles that reveal a quote or illustration.
5. No question uses three versions of the same mechanic. The rotation should make the feed feel varied without adding complicated rules.
6. Feedback should explain why the best answer is more complete than the distractors.
7. Personal reflection prompts connect the teaching to ordinary life without requesting sexual history, relationship secrets, trauma, or other intimate disclosure.
8. Each scored quiz or game has one scored attempt. Afterward, the correct solution and rationale are shown.

## Approved question flow

Every question follows this vertical order:

1. **Reading** — one horizontal slide per official text. This remains unchanged.
2. **Quiz** — one demanding multiple-choice scenario. All options should be plausible; the user must select the most complete answer.
3. **Personal reflection** — one non-invasive question about what the teaching could mean in ordinary personal life.
   - Before submission: show the writing prompt and input.
   - After submission: replace the input screen with the global reflections overview.
   - Show reflections from all groups together.
   - Do not display names, ages, group names, or other author identifiers.
   - Keep the author’s internal user ID private so the system can award XP without revealing identity on the board.
   - A participant cannot heart their own reflection and can heart another reflection only once.
   - Each heart received awards the reflection’s author **+5 XP**.
4. **Games** — four games per question, rotating the five mechanics below.
5. **Overview** — show:
   - XP this participant contributed to their group through this question.
   - Total XP earned by the participant’s group through this question.
   - A clear invitation to continue to the next question.

There is no separate reflections section after the games; it is integrated into the personal-reflection step.

### Mechanic notes

- **Crossword:** 4–6 short doctrinal clues. A small word bank can be shown on mobile so difficulty comes from understanding the clue, not spelling in English or Portuguese.
- **Match pairs:** three concept-and-meaning pairs with deliberately close meanings.
- **Reveal cards:** reveal one situation or statement at a time, then classify it or choose the most fitting response before seeing the explanation.
- **Set into the right order:** drag 3–4 steps into a meaningful sequence. The order should express a logic, not merely chronology. The starting position should never be the actual order.
- **Move blocks:** slide or rearrange blocks to reconstruct a quote or illustration. The completed puzzle reveals the full image, source, and one short learning statement.

Suggested review method: edit the wording directly and add comments under **Editor notes** for each question.

## Question 3 — Can love last?

Official question: *Isn’t love fleeting—something that comes and goes, passing from one love interest to another?*

### Learning objective

Distinguish attraction and emotion from the decision, fidelity, habits, and grace through which love can mature.

### Game 1 — Set into the right order: From attraction to mature love

Prompt: **A relationship begins with strong attraction. Put the next stages of mature love in the most coherent order.**

1. Notice attraction and the desire for closeness.
2. See the real person beyond idealization.
3. Choose the other person’s good in concrete decisions.
4. Build patterns of fidelity that can survive changing feelings.

Rationale: Feelings can begin love’s movement, but mature love requires reality, decision, and repeated fidelity.

### Game 2 — Reveal cards: What begins, sustains, or distorts love?

Prompt: **Reveal each card and place it under “Begins love,” “Sustains love,” or “Distorts love.”**

- Strong attraction → Begins love.
- Seeing the real person beyond idealization → Sustains love.
- Repeatedly choosing the other person’s good → Sustains love.
- Treating a person as replaceable when excitement changes → Distorts love.
- Remaining while refusing to name serious harm → Distorts love.

Completion insight: Mature love holds attraction, truth, decision, and fidelity together.

### Game 3 — Move blocks: Reveal the central sentence

Puzzle: Rearrange a small set of crooked word blocks to reconstruct:

> **LOVE / IS A / DECISION / WITHOUT / REPEAL**

Reward reveal:

- Full quote: “Love is a decision without repeal.”
- Source label: YOUCAT Love Forever 3.
- Illustration: John and Isabelle choosing one shared path while several tempting side paths remain visible behind them.

Completion insight: The sentence does not deny feelings; it says that lasting love cannot be governed by feelings alone.

### Game 4 — Crossword: What helps love remain?

Use a word bank containing: **FEELING, REALITY, DECISION, FIDELITY, GRACE**.

Clues:

1. An important experience that can change and cannot carry lasting love alone. → FEELING
2. Seeing the actual person beyond idealization. → REALITY
3. The use of freedom that gives love direction. → DECISION
4. Love practiced repeatedly across changing circumstances. → FIDELITY
5. God’s help strengthening limited human love. → GRACE

### Quiz — One scored scenario

Prompt: **After two years, a couple feels less excitement than at the beginning. Which response best takes love seriously?**

A. Create constant novelty so that the original intensity never has to change.

B. Talk honestly, examine the health of the relationship, and choose concrete acts of care instead of treating lower intensity as automatic failure. **[Correct]**

C. Remain together at any cost, because staying is always proof of love.

Feedback: Love does not depend on maintaining infatuation, but fidelity also does not require denying serious problems. Truth and concrete care belong together.

### Personal reflection

**Which ordinary action could help another person experience you as reliable this week?**

## Question 14 — Sex and the truth of self-gift

Official question: *Why can’t I just have sex with whoever I want?*

### Learning objective

Understand that the body expresses the person and that consent, love, permanence, and the possibility of new life cannot be reduced to a merely physical transaction.

### Game 1 — Match pairs: Necessary, but not identical

- Attraction → Can awaken desire without yet establishing a total gift.
- Consent → Is indispensable, but by itself does not create permanence or a shared future.
- Marriage covenant → Gives a public and lasting form to the complete mutual gift.

### Game 2 — Crossword: The language of the body

Use a word bank containing: **BODY, SOUL, DIGNITY, GIFT, COVENANT**.

Clues:

1. The visible part of the person that can express an invisible intention. → BODY
2. The spiritual dimension that shows why a person is never “only physical.” → SOUL
3. What prevents another person from becoming a means to an end. → DIGNITY
4. Something freely given that cannot be reduced to a transaction. → GIFT
5. The lasting promise that gives total self-gift a truthful home. → COVENANT

Completion insight: Sex and love belong together as body and soul belong together.

### Game 3 — Reveal cards: What does this action communicate?

Prompt: **Reveal each card. Decide whether the action communicates “partial closeness,” “total self-gift,” or “contradiction.”**

- Mutual attraction without a lasting promise → Partial closeness.
- Free consent and genuine care, but an explicit refusal of permanence → Partial closeness; something real is present, but not a total self-gift.
- A free, exclusive, lasting covenant expressed bodily → Total self-gift.
- Using another person mainly for excitement, status, comfort, or escape → Contradiction.

Completion insight: Consent and affection are essential, but Christian sexual ethics also asks whether the bodily act tells the truth about the whole gift of the person.

### Game 4 — Set into the right order: When bodily language becomes truthful

Prompt: **Put these elements in the order that makes a total bodily gift truthful.**

1. Recognize the other person as someone with inviolable dignity.
2. Discern whether love is exclusive, responsible, and open to a shared future.
3. Make a free and lasting covenant.
4. Express that complete gift through bodily union.

### Quiz — One scored scenario

Prompt: **Two adults care for each other and freely consent to sex, but neither intends permanence. Which assessment is most complete?**

A. Consent is important, but it does not by itself make the bodily language of total self-gift correspond to a total promise. **[Correct]**

B. Their care is irrelevant because only legal status matters.

C. Their consent settles every moral question because no other meaning belongs to sex.

Feedback: Consent and affection matter deeply. The Christian claim is that sexual union also speaks a language of complete, exclusive, and lasting self-gift.

### Personal reflection

**What helps your actions communicate the same truth as your intentions?**

## Question 25 — Spiritual direction and freedom

Official question: *People tell me I should find a spiritual director. What is that?*

### Learning objective

Recognize spiritual direction as accompaniment that helps someone read life in the light of the Gospel while preserving freedom and responsibility.

### Game 1 — Move blocks: Reveal an Emmaus-style illustration

Puzzle: Slide image blocks into place to reveal a sparse illustration of two people walking while a wise companion listens, walks beside them, and points gently toward an open Gospel.

Revealed sentence:

> **A good guide listens, liberates, and encourages.**

Completion insight: The guide accompanies the path; the guide does not take ownership of the other person’s vocation.

### Game 2 — Set into the right order: A healthy discernment process

Prompt: **Put these parts of a good spiritual-direction conversation in order.**

1. Bring concrete experience, desires, fears, and decisions honestly.
2. Listen and interpret them in the light of prayer and the Gospel.
3. Test possible paths without surrendering personal responsibility.
4. Decide freely and later review the fruits of the decision.

### Game 3 — Match pairs: Similar actions, different effects

- Wise challenge → Names something difficult while still respecting freedom.
- Manipulation → Uses pressure, fear, or authority to control the decision.
- Healthy accompaniment → Helps the person become more truthful, prayerful, and responsible.

### Game 4 — Crossword: Signs of good accompaniment

Use a word bank containing: **LISTEN, GOSPEL, FREEDOM, VOCATION, FRUIT**.

Clues:

1. What a guide must do before interpreting or advising. → LISTEN
2. The light in which experience and desire are discerned. → GOSPEL
3. What good direction protects rather than replaces. → FREEDOM
4. The call that belongs to the person before God, not to the director. → VOCATION
5. The result over time that helps test whether direction is healthy. → FRUIT

### Quiz — One scored scenario

Prompt: **A spiritual director says, “I am certain this is the person you must marry.” What is the most mature response?**

A. Obey immediately, because a director carries responsibility for the decision.

B. Receive the advice seriously, but continue praying, testing, and deciding freely; a director must not replace conscience or vocation. **[Correct]**

C. End spiritual direction, because a guide should never offer a clear opinion.

Feedback: A director may challenge or advise strongly, but cannot take over the person’s responsibility before God.

### Personal reflection

**In what kind of decision could a wise outside perspective help you see more clearly without deciding for you?**

## Question 34 — Freedom in a sexualized digital environment

Official question: *What am I supposed to do about all the sexual images around me? Look away?*

### Learning objective

Move beyond vague willpower toward a concrete strategy involving interruption, environment, accountability, prayer, and the possibility of a new start.

### Game 1 — Reveal cards: Choose the strongest first move

Prompt: **Reveal one situation at a time and choose the strongest immediate response before seeing the explanation.**

- An unwanted sexual reel appears unexpectedly → Close it immediately rather than testing how long you can resist.
- You are alone, tired, and returning to a known trigger → Change place and interrupt isolation before relying on willpower.
- The same pattern has returned several times → Tell a trusted person and strengthen the plan or filter.
- You have fallen again → Refuse both denial and despair; seek confession, review the trigger, and begin again.
- An explicit meme appears in a group chat → Stop the exposure, do not forward it, and set a clear boundary without humiliating the sender.

### Game 2 — Crossword: Build a strategy for freedom

Use a word bank containing: **TRIGGER, PLAN, FRIEND, PRAYER, FILTER**.

Clues:

1. A recurring situation that should be recognized before it becomes overwhelming. → TRIGGER
2. A decision made while calm for use when willpower is weaker. → PLAN
3. A trusted person who breaks isolation and helps accountability. → FRIEND
4. Turning toward God for peace, strength, mercy, and a new start. → PRAYER
5. A technical support that is useful but does not replace conversion. → FILTER

### Game 3 — Set into the right order: When an unwanted image appears

Prompt: **Put these actions in the strongest practical order.**

1. Interrupt the image or page immediately.
2. Change the physical or digital situation instead of remaining isolated with the trigger.
3. Contact a trusted person when the temptation remains strong.
4. Pray, learn from the trigger, and adjust the plan for next time.

### Game 4 — Move blocks: Reveal a concrete freedom plan

Puzzle: Reconstruct an illustration of John turning away from a screen, moving out of an isolated room, contacting a friend, and returning toward prayer and community.

The completed illustration reveals five small symbols:

1. Close.
2. Move.
3. Tell.
4. Pray.
5. Begin again.

Completion insight: Freedom is easier to protect when the plan is concrete before the vulnerable moment arrives.

### Quiz — One scored scenario

Prompt: **A friend posts an explicit meme in a group chat. Which response best protects freedom without humiliating anyone?**

A. Keep looking long enough to understand the joke, then silently delete it.

B. Stop the exposure, avoid forwarding it, set a clear boundary or report it when needed, and speak privately if that could help. **[Correct]**

C. Publicly shame the sender so that everyone understands the seriousness.

Feedback: Freedom requires decisive action, but the dignity of the sender also matters. Clear boundaries need not become humiliation.

### Personal reflection

**Which small change in your digital environment would make your freedom easier to protect?**

## Question 59 — Friendship, attraction, and truthful boundaries

Official question: *Is it really possible for a man and a woman to be friends without romantic feelings developing?*

### Learning objective

Avoid both suspicion of every friendship and naïve denial of attraction; practice self-knowledge, honesty, transparency, and boundaries.

### Game 1 — Match pairs: What does this behavior communicate?

- Warm, transparent friendship → Seeks the other’s good without creating a hidden claim.
- Repeated ambiguous flirting → Enjoys closeness while leaving expectations unclear.
- A truthful boundary → May limit closeness in order to protect both people’s freedom.

### Game 2 — Move blocks: Reveal an illustration about transparency

Puzzle: Reconstruct an illustration of John and Isabelle talking as friends in an open, ordinary setting. A third friend can enter naturally; nothing needs to be hidden.

Revealed question:

> **Would this friendship still feel truthful if someone else walked into the room?**

Completion insight: Privacy can be healthy; secrecy that protects ambiguity is a warning sign.

### Game 3 — Reveal cards: Transparent, ambiguous, or boundary needed?

Prompt: **Reveal one behavior at a time and place it under “Transparent,” “Ambiguous,” or “Boundary needed.”**

- Warm friendship that can be discussed openly with others → Transparent.
- Repeated flirting while insisting that it means nothing → Ambiguous.
- Private late-night closeness that competes with an existing commitment → Boundary needed.
- Acknowledging attraction and reducing intimacy so that nobody receives false hope → Transparent boundary.
- Enjoying another person’s attention while avoiding a clear answer → Ambiguous.

### Game 4 — Crossword: Friendship with truth and freedom

Use a word bank containing: **HONESTY, ATTRACTION, BOUNDARY, FREEDOM, FRIENDSHIP**.

Clues:

1. Naming what is actually happening instead of protecting ambiguity. → HONESTY
2. A real feeling that need not destroy friendship but should not be denied. → ATTRACTION
3. A limit chosen to protect the good of both people. → BOUNDARY
4. The ability to seek the other’s good without creating a hidden claim. → FREEDOM
5. A relationship that sees and values the whole person. → FRIENDSHIP

### Quiz — One scored scenario

Prompt: **Two friends message late every night. One begins dating someone else, but the private intensity continues. What is the most loving response?**

A. Continue as before because friendship should never be limited by another relationship.

B. Speak honestly about what the closeness communicates and adjust the pattern if it undermines clarity or another commitment. **[Correct]**

C. End the friendship immediately, because attraction makes friendship impossible.

Feedback: Friendship can remain real, but transparency and boundaries may be needed when private closeness creates ambiguity or competes with another commitment.

### Personal reflection

**What habit helps your friendships remain clear, respectful, and free?**

## Question 83 — Cohabitation, momentum, and a free promise

Official question: *Why can’t we just move in together?*

### Learning objective

Distinguish practical convenience and gradual momentum from the free, public, unconditional covenant that gives shared life its foundation.

### Game 1 — Move blocks: Are we choosing or drifting?

Puzzle: Reconstruct an illustration of John and Isabelle carrying moving boxes. One route is a conveyor belt labelled by symbols of convenience and momentum; the other route pauses at a freely chosen covenant before reaching a shared home.

Revealed question:

> **Are we freely choosing our future—or simply following the easiest next step?**

Completion insight: Practical circumstances matter, but they should not silently make the marriage decision.

### Game 2 — Reveal cards: Pressure, discernment, or covenant?

Prompt: **Reveal each reason for moving in and identify what it actually answers: “Real practical pressure,” “Relationship discernment,” or “Covenant.”**

- “Two rents have become unaffordable.” → Real practical pressure.
- “We want to see how each person handles conflict and responsibility.” → Relationship discernment, though cohabitation is not the only way to learn this.
- “We have freely promised our whole future publicly.” → Covenant.
- “Breaking up would now be too complicated.” → Momentum that may weaken freedom.
- “Everyone assumes this is simply the next stage.” → Social momentum, not covenant.

### Game 3 — Crossword: What turns a house into a common home?

Use a word bank containing: **PROMISE, FREEDOM, MOMENTUM, COVENANT, HOME**.

Clues:

1. The definitive word that should come before the total shared life. → PROMISE
2. The capacity to choose rather than drift into a decision. → FREEDOM
3. Movement created by dependence and habit even when no clear decision was made. → MOMENTUM
4. A public, unconditional, lifelong bond. → COVENANT
5. Shared life as the expression of the promise, not a trial replacing it. → HOME

### Game 4 — Match pairs: What can shape the decision?

- Practical pressure → Can be serious without answering whether a lifelong covenant has been chosen.
- Emotional and sexual bonding → Can deepen closeness while making unresolved problems harder to evaluate freely.
- Public promise → Makes the couple’s unconditional decision visible and accountable.

### Quiz — One scored scenario

C. If they truly love each other, practical circumstances should never influence their decisions.

Feedback: Practical pressure deserves a practical response, but it should not silently replace the free decision and public covenant of marriage.

## Question 127 — Fidelity, boundaries, and safety in crisis

Official question: *How can I promise to be faithful “in good times and bad” when I don’t know how difficult those times will be (alcohol, drugs, infidelity)?*

### Learning objective

Hold together unconditional love, truth, wise help, boundaries, and the duty to protect life and dignity. Fidelity is not denial or cooperation with destruction.

### Game 1 — Reveal cards: Identify the first faithful priority

Prompt: **Reveal one crisis at a time and choose the first priority before seeing the full response.**

- Violence or an immediate threat to children → Safety first; leave danger and involve qualified help.
- A hidden addiction with repeated consequences → Truth, professional help, and boundaries that do not enable it.
- Infidelity is discovered without immediate danger → Stabilize, seek wise support, establish truth and boundaries, then discern the next steps.
- One spouse asks the family to hide destructive behavior → Refuse secrecy that protects the behavior rather than the person.
- A crisis has become too complex for friends alone → Involve professional, legal, and pastoral competence appropriate to the situation.

### Game 2 — Crossword: Fidelity in a grave crisis

Use a word bank containing: **SAFETY, TRUTH, HELP, BOUNDARY, FIDELITY**.

Clues:

1. The first priority when life or dignity is seriously threatened. → SAFETY
2. What breaks the secrecy in which addiction and abuse often grow. → TRUTH
3. What should be qualified and appropriate to the crisis, not improvised by one person alone. → HELP
4. A limit that protects the person and refuses cooperation with destruction. → BOUNDARY
5. Love that refuses both abandonment of the person and denial of reality. → FIDELITY

### Game 3 — Set into the right order: Responding to a grave crisis

Prompt: **Put these priorities in the most responsible order when addiction, violence, or serious danger appears.**

1. Protect immediate life and safety, including the safety of children.
2. Tell the truth and involve qualified professional, legal, and pastoral help.
3. Establish boundaries that do not enable destructive behavior.
4. Discern the next faithful step, which may include living apart.

### Game 4 — Match pairs: Distinctions that matter

- Unconditional love → Refuses to treat the person as disposable.
- A firm boundary → Refuses to cooperate with behavior that destroys the person or family.
- Separation for safety → Can protect life and dignity without declaring the person unloved.

### Quiz — One scored scenario

Prompt: **A spouse repeatedly hides an addiction and asks the family to cover the consequences. What is the most faithful response?**

A. Protect the spouse’s reputation by solving the consequences privately.

B. Tell the truth, refuse to enable the addiction, seek qualified help, and establish proportionate boundaries. **[Correct]**

C. End every form of relationship immediately, because trust has already been broken.

Feedback: Loyalty to the person can require resistance to destructive behavior. Covering consequences may strengthen the addiction rather than help the person.

### Personal reflection

**How can someone remain loyal to a person without cooperating with destructive behavior?**

## Question 140 — When love changes over time

Official question: *Doesn’t all love fade with time?*

### Learning objective

Recognize that love may change from infatuation into a truer and deeper communion through decision, grace, gratitude, forgiveness, respect, and prayer.

### Game 1 — Match pairs: The task of each season

- Celebration → Receive the good with gratitude instead of entitlement.
- Routine → Notice and choose the person who has become familiar.
- Crisis → Join truth, forgiveness, help, and concrete change.

### Game 2 — Move blocks: Reveal the promise of mature love

Puzzle: Reconstruct an illustration of John and Isabelle at two stages of life: youthful first love on one side and the same couple walking together in old age on the other.

Word blocks form:

> **LOVE / NEVER / ENDS**

Source label: 1 Corinthians 13:8.

Completion insight: Natural love may change and weaken, but it can also become more truthful, forgiving, respectful, and deeply rooted in God’s love.

### Game 3 — Reveal cards: Fading, maturing, or warning sign?

Prompt: **Reveal one sign at a time and place it under “Maturing,” “Needs attention,” or “Not enough information.”**

- Emotional intensity is lower, but gratitude and truthful care are growing → Maturing.
- A couple avoids every difficult conversation so that peace is never disturbed → Needs attention.
- Routine has increased → Not enough information; routine can hide neglect or become a place of faithful care.
